# Arpent

The arpent is a historic unit of measure used primarily in France and in French colonial areas such as Quebec and Louisiana. It was used both as a unit of length and as a unit of area for land measurement.

## Definition

- As a unit of length, the arpent varied by region but typically represented a distance between roughly 50 and 70 metres (depending on the local "French foot").
- As a unit of area, an arpent often described a plot of land roughly equivalent to about 0.34 hectares (about 0.85 acres), though values could differ regionally.

## Brief history

The arpent dates back to medieval France and was commonly used in surveying and land division. French settlers brought the measure to North America, where it persisted in legal documents and cadastral records long after other units became standard.

## Common uses

- Describing farm lots, especially the long rectangular parcels found along rivers in Quebec and Louisiana.
- Appearing in historical property deeds, census records, and maps.

## Legacy

Today the arpent is mostly of historical interest. It still appears in place names, archival documents, and discussions of historical land use. Converting arpents to modern units is often necessary when interpreting old records.

## References

- Historical measurement references and local land registry guides can provide precise conversion factors for a given region and period.
